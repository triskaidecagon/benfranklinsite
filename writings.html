Famous Writings

  • Franklin’s first published writings were under the penname “Mrs. Silence Dogood.” He was working on his Brother James’s printing press at the time, but James wouldn’t publish any of his writings. So, Franklin took up the penname and wrote letters to his brother’s newspaper.
  • The first pamphlet that he published was named “A Dissertation upon Liberty and Necessity, Pleasure and Pain.” In it, he made the argument that free will is nonexistent, and that means we aren’t morally responsible for our actions.
  • In 1732, Franklin published Poor Richards Almanack. It had information about the upcoming weather, along with a variety of other things, like proverbs. He would publish a new volume every year for twenty five years.
  • Franklin was the owner of the Pennsylvania Gazette, which was a newspaper. Under pseudonyms, he contributed a lot of content to it. During the French and Indian war, he drew and published a cartoon for it, named “Join or Die.” It stressed that the colonies needed to join together, or else they would all fall.
