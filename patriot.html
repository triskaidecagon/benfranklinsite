Contributions to America

  • Franklin proposed the Albany Plan, which was a plan to unite the colonies. Although it was rejected, it would become the groundwork for the Articles of Confederation, which was America’s first government.
  • He debated against the Stamp Act in Parliament, which was in London. Franklin’s testimony against it led to its end in 1766.
  • Franklin was a delegate at the Second Continental Congress. He helped draft the Declaration of Independence, and helped persuade France to help America out in the Revolutionary War.
  • Franklin also helped negotiate the Treaty of Paris with Britain. The Treaty of Paris ended the Revolutionary War, recognized America as an independent nation, and set its borders.
