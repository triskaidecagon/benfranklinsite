Famous Inventions and Experiments

  • Franklin did many experiments with electricity. His famous “Key and Kite” experiment, which he performed in 1752, helped prove that lightning is electricity. He also invented the lightning rod, which protected buildings from lightning, and created many terms related to electricity that we now use today.
  • He made a musical instrument, the glass armonica, too. It was made of spinning glass bowls on a shaft. Beethoven and Mozart both composed music for it.
  • Franklin invented Bifocals, glasses that can be used for seeing distant objects and for reading.
  • Another invention that Franklin created was the Franklin Stove. It created more heat with less fuel.
  • He invented a scheme that would drop the letters C, J, Q, W, X and Y from the alphabet. Why? He felt that it would help reduce redundancy.
  • Franklin is also credited with inventing the rocking chair, a flexible catheter, and the American penny, along with many more things.
